What we measure

Five markers. Agreed in week one. Retested in week twelve.

No vague promises. Every engagement is held to five markers you agree at the start and see retested at the end. That is what turns a programme into accountability.

01

Sleep efficiency

Time asleep against time in bed, measured by your wearable.

02

Resting heart rate

The lowest sustained overnight value, tracked as a weekly average.

03

Body composition

Lean mass, fat mass, or waist measure, depending on your tier.

04

Daytime energy

A subjective score logged twice daily across the engagement.

05

Performance marker

Grip, a VO2 estimate, a key lift, or a blood marker. Agreed in week one.

Questions

Before you enquire.

Do I need blood tests?
Not for Starter or Core. Those track the five markers using your wearable and simple measures. Premium includes a biomarker panel. If anything points to a clinical question at any tier, you are referred to the right professional.
Can I travel during the programme?
Yes. The protocol is built to survive a heavy travel and meeting schedule. Calls are held by video and support is asynchronous, so the work continues wherever you are.
Do you work with clients outside the UK?
Yes for the self-guided product and Core, which run entirely by video and message. Premium includes in-person sessions, currently held in London.
Which wearables work?
Most modern wearables are fine, including Oura, Whoop, Apple Watch and Garmin. If you do not already use one, that is sorted in week one.
How much time does this take each week?
A 30-minute weekly call, plus short daily inputs. The protocol is designed to reduce friction and fit your schedule, not to add a second job.
Is this medical care?
No. This is an education, coaching and performance programme. It does not diagnose, treat or manage illness. Where something sits outside that scope, you are referred to a suitably qualified professional.
What if I already have a trainer or a doctor?
That is common, and welcome. The role here is to run the whole picture and coordinate the parts, your training and your clinical care included, rather than to replace anyone.
